About

Turning analysis and prototyping into real hardware.

I’m a Mechanical Engineering student specializing in Mechatronics at the University of New Brunswick. My work focuses on using modeling and simulation to drive design decisions in vehicles and mechanical systems, then validating those decisions through lab work and prototyping. I’ve worked on FSAE suspension and wheel-load modeling, manufacturing labs (forging, rolling, drawing), and numerical methods in Python and MATLAB.

Quick highlights
  • FSAE: wheel-load, roll, and ride frequency analysis in MATLAB/Simulink.
  • Manufacturing: forging and drawing experiments with 6061-T6 aluminum and steel.
  • Numerical methods: iterative solvers, root finding, LU factorization in Python/MATLAB.
  • Cycling/mechanics: hands-on experience with bicycle assembly, maintenance, and component upgrades.
